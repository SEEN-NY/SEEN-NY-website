01 — About

Who We Are

SEEN NY is a nonprofit organization dedicated to economic justice through anti-racist systems change. We support Black and Afro-Latino/a/e communities in building ownership and power within New York State's cannabis industry through cooperative economics.

02 — Why

The Case for Action

Cannabis legalization in New York State creates a critical opportunity for equity, ownership, and economic repair.

The history of cannabis prohibition is rooted in racial bias and has been used as a tool of systemic oppression against Black and Afro-Latino/a/e communities. As legalization expands, the industry sits directly at the intersection of social and economic justice.

SEEN NY works to ensure this moment leads to structural change—not repetition of harm.

We educate, advocate, and build coalitions grounded in anti-racist systems change to support meaningful participation in the cannabis economy.

04 — Cooperative

The Cooperative Cannabis Project

A long-term effort to build a cooperative ecosystem on the distribution side of the cannabis supply chain. That means farmer-owned, worker-owned, community-owned businesses.

We are starting with access to knowledge and capital — an incubator.

What the Incubator Delivers

  • 12–16 months of hands-on, high-touch support
  • Cannabis-specific education & technical training
  • Cooperative business planning and financial preparation
  • Democratic governance & conflict resolution
  • Compliance coaching
Join us in this historic, high-touch, first-of-its-kind incubator for New York's first worker-owned cannabis processor, led by people from Communities Disproportionately Impacted (CDIs).
